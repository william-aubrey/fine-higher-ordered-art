William Ryan Aubrey, 51 years old. Has lived in Charlotte NC since 1999, painting since ~2003 when he moved into his current home. Over 100 paintings total; 52 selected as the core "Charlotte Collection."

Owns land in the mountains and has been working the property on weekends for years. Goal is to build a cabin and move there permanently. May have enough money without selling paintings, but wants the financial security margin. The paintings are deeply personal — he will only sell them if the auction meaningfully moves the needle toward funding the cabin and move.

Previously gave away paintings and let some sell at charity auctions; stopped because people didn't value what was given freely. This experience directly shapes the premium pricing tiers and the all-or-nothing auction structure.

Has secured a $250,000 HELOC against his Charlotte home to fund cabin construction. Plans to start building spring 2026. The auction is supplemental financial security, not the sole funding source.

Considers himself essentially retired from painting at this point.

Originally attached to keeping the 6-painting Grief Series together as a set. During the 2026-03-15 sprint, worked through this attachment and decided to drop the structural constraint — the aggregate threshold alone protects against selling cheap. Described the moment as experiencing his own "Acceptance" (Grief step 5) through the design process.
