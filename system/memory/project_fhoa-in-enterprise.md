FHOA is one of four enterprise instances governed by the C3PO architecture: Trading Card Co (digital collectibles), Mountain Cabin Co (residential construction), Fine Higher Ordered Art (art auction), and Ouborobo (enterprise-as-a-service). FHOA was the fourth onboarded and the first by automated `/onboard` command.

**Why:** The C3PO 3rd edition whitepaper (read in full during SPR-008, ~44k words) reveals the enterprise operates across three tiers: a portfolio of autonomous businesses generating wealth, partnerships extending the architecture to other humans, and 3-4 community businesses kept because they serve people the architect cares about. FHOA is explicitly in the community tier — kept not for portfolio returns but because "the paintings find the people who need them through a mechanism that rewards courage over wealth."

**How to apply:** FHOA's design decisions should prioritize meaning over optimization. The auction mechanism rewards courage (low-tier bidders get outbid rewards, sequential tier progression), not wealth maximization. When facing trade-offs between revenue efficiency and the spirit of the soul document, lean toward the soul. The Principal's relationship to these paintings is personal — they are expressions of emotional and spiritual themes from his life, not inventory.

## Theoretical Context

The whitepaper grounds the architecture in:
- **Gödel's incompleteness** — the system that models cannot be the system being modeled
- **Ashby's requisite variety** — the regulator must be external to achieve requisite variety
- **Hofstadter's strange loops** — the DAB failure (summer 2025) was a strange loop; C3PO resolves it through separation
- **Boundary-enforcement** — compliance measured at the A-0 interface, not inside the instance; the corporate veil as design principle

The 20:1 work-to-judgment ratio identified in SPR-006 is validated by the whitepaper's core claim: the bottleneck is judgment clarity, not execution capacity.
